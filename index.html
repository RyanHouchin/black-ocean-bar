Method

  1. Toast cacao nibs in a dry skillet, 30 seconds, until fragrant. Set aside.
  2. Brown the butter over medium heat until golden and nutty. Pull off heat. Stir in ghee and cashew butter.
  3. Let cool to warm (~110F). Dissolve shilajit in the warm fat.
  4. Vitamix: warm fat mix + raw liver + egg yolks + raw honey + royal jelly + vanilla + lemon juice. Blend until completely smooth.
  5. Whisk all dry ingredients in a separate bowl.
  6. Pour wet into dry. Mix until a thick, cohesive dough forms.
  7. Fold in toasted cacao nibs and gelatin powder.
  8. Scoop and roll into balls. Each ball should weigh ~35g on the scale.
  9. Roll each ball in cacao powder first, then dust with strawberry powder.
  10. Press 3-4 Maldon salt flakes into the top of each ball.
  11. Freeze 10 minutes to set the shell. Transfer to fridge one hour.

Notes

Scale target
Each ball should read ~35g on a kitchen scale. That's a 1.5" truffle.
Too crumbly
Add ghee, 5g at a time.
Too wet
Add whey, 10g at a time.
Raw liver
Vitamix liquifies completely. Fats and cacao mask any taste.
Lemon juice
Classic pâté technique. Brightens iron and mineral notes.
Royal jelly
Add at warm stage (~110F) to preserve enzymes.
Shilajit
Warm the jar in hot water. Wet spoon. Dissolves instantly in warm fat.
Toast the nibs
Unlocks volatile aromatics. Makes chocolate three-dimensional.
Espresso
Standard pastry technique. Amplifies cacao without adding coffee flavor.
Cayenne
Mexican chocolate tradition. Creates warmth that extends the finish.
Salt on top
Discrete crystals create sweet/salt/chocolate contrast. Never mix in.
Freeze first
10 min freeze sets the shell. Fridge softens the core. Truffle mouthfeel.
Whey varies
Check your label: grams protein per gram powder. Adjust totals accordingly.
